Benjamin Franklin

by Walter Isaacson

Benjamin Franklin, an ambitious entrepreneur and self-made man, was a key Founding Father who, through his innovations as a scientist, inventor, diplomat, and writer, significantly shaped both his era and the enduring American character. He uniquely influenced all foundational U.S. documents and championed democratic values, remaining a resonant figure today.
